# Ontology

## 1. Surface Reality

The work takes place in a sealed, concrete location that presents as a snowbound hospice annex. It is materially legible: rooms, records, staff traces, personal effects, intake documents, maintenance habits.

## 2. Hidden Reality

The annex is also a quarantined chamber for adjudication. It exists to pressure contradiction into coherence. It cannot sustain all possibilities indefinitely.

## 3. The Reader

The reader believes they are investigating. In fact, they are judging. Their interpretive commitment has ontological force.

## 4. The Claimants

Claimants are not merely suspects. Each claimant is a pressure toward a specific world-logic. To accuse one is to strengthen that logic and starve rival realities.

## 5. The Central Transgression

A patient or resident was moved, released, or signed out under impossible or contradictory circumstances. The transgression is concrete on the surface and metaphysical underneath.

## 6. Action Semantics

- Accusation selects a reality and collapses ambiguity.
- Retraction weakens a chosen reality but leaves scars.
- Indecision preserves plurality temporarily but increases contamination when prolonged.
- Exhaustive traversal without commitment may mark the reader as parasitic or adjudically suspect.

## 7. Mutation Law

The place mutates in response to judgment pressure. Environmental details, documents, naming conventions, room function, and tone shift toward the ontology of the dominant claimant.

## 8. Contamination

Contamination is the cost of prolonged noncommittal consumption. It appears when the reader tries to extract all possible truths without accepting the cost of judgment.

## 9. Observables vs Secrets

Observable:

- documents
- rooms
- interviews
- contradictions
- regime drift

Hidden:

- full metaphysical explanation
- the system’s original purpose
- the deeper identity of the adjudicated force
- whether the reader is being tested, used, or diagnosed
