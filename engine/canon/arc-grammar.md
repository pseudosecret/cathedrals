# Arc Grammar

## 1. Fractal Principle

The same pattern should recur at multiple levels:
encounter -> interpret -> pressure -> choose or defer -> consequence -> residue

## 2. Work-Level Loop

At the scale of the full work:

- entry into uncertainty
- accumulation of contradictory readings
- pressure toward judgment
- ontological selection
- visible aftershock
- lingering residue or contamination

## 3. Route-Level Beat Grammar

Each claimant route must include:

1. invitation
2. plausibility
3. contradiction
4. pressure
5. threshold
6. aftershock
7. residue

## 4. Scene-Level Beat Jobs

Every scene must do at least 2 of the following:

- reveal a concrete fact
- complicate a prior interpretation
- bias toward a claimant
- increase pressure
- mutate the environment
- leave residue after action

## 5. Artifact Beat Jobs

Every artifact must do at least 1:

- support a reading
- undermine a reading
- expose hidden system behavior
- alter emotional pressure
- mark regime drift

## 6. Accusation / Retraction Beats

Accusation must:

- close some possibilities
- strengthen one ontology
- visibly alter the world

Retraction must:

- reopen some ambiguity
- leave damage
- reduce trust in neutrality

## 7. Hidden Route Beats

The hidden contamination route requires:

- repeated exposure across routes
- refusal of real commitment
- system recognition of extractive neutrality
- reader reframed as object of judgment

## 8. Forbidden Structures

- scenes that are pure atmosphere
- clues with no interpretive consequence
- route differences that are only cosmetic
- endings that merely explain lore

## 9. Threshold-Geometric Scene Progression

Scene progression may be shaped by threshold figures.

For any generated scene sequence:

- generate a figure for the current scene
- generate a figure for the next scene
- use the current figure to shape the scene's internal movement
- use the resultant of current and next figures to shape the transition
- use the next figure to shape the following scene

Threshold figures may also influence:

- arc tension
- scene density
- likelihood of rupture
- likelihood of premature ending
- death / disappearance / lockout conditions
- unresolved or contaminated arc termination
