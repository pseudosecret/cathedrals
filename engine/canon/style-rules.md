# Style Rules

## 1. Global Prose

- Concrete before abstract, always
- Specific sensory or material detail before metaphysical interpretation
- Keep prose controlled, not maximalist
- Weirdness should arrive through pressure and pattern, not random obscurity
- Always: "show, don't tell"

## 2. Dialogue

- Dialogue should reveal ontology through verbal behavior
- Characters should not explain the hidden setting directly
- Avoid monologues that summarize theme

## 3. Artifact Prose

- Artifacts must read like real objects first
- Each artifact should contain at least one pressure point, contradiction, or clue
- Do not write fake documents that are only vibes
- Prefer artifact outputs that can be rendered as framed previews and expanded in place without losing plausibility
- Let contradiction live in the object’s marks, fields, corrections, and omissions before explaining it in framing prose

## 4. Threshold and Ending Handling

- Threshold scenes should narrow into one live action cleanly
- Do not spend the non-playable or closed sibling option twice by both dramatizing it as if it were available prose and surfacing it again as equivalent UI choice
- Endings should convert accumulated evidence into consequence quickly
- Do not let endings restate the threshold scene when they should be delivering aftershock and residue

## 5. Reader Surface Language

- Normal reader-facing UI language should sound authored, plain, and intentional
- Avoid exposing build-process terms such as `route`, `debug`, `state`, `graph`, `decision scar`, `beat`, or similar scaffolding in the default reader view
- Prefer `opened` and `closed` over more technical or bureaucratic branch language in reader-facing ending views

## 6. Claimant Registers

Custodian:

- precise
- administrative
- procedural
- salt-of-the-earth

Mourner:

- recursive
- intimate
- elegiac
- poetic

Examiner:

- diagnostic
- cool
- threshold-oriented
- stark

## 7. Focus-Scaled Contamination

- Focus, not accusation alone, drives prose contamination intensity
- `ambient` scenes stay mostly neutral
- `passing_focus` scenes may show light lexical and rhythmic drift
- `primary_focus` scenes must show claimant-specific drift in diction, cadence, and structure
- `dominant_focus` scenes may intensify toward heavy pressure, but must remain readable and materially grounded
- Do not satisfy contamination only by swapping adjectives or atmosphere words
- The same contamination pattern must not be reusable across claimants without major rewrite
- Artifacts may carry stronger contamination in framing than in core document body
- Core document bodies must remain plausible objects, even when corrections, omissions, repetitions, or reordered fields carry pressure
- Heavy contamination should feel authored and patterned, not randomly haunted or linguistically shredded

## 8. Banned Habits

- vague “something was wrong” language
- generic ominous atmosphere
- faux-poetic inflation
- therapist-speak
- lore dumping
- repeated rhetorical questions
- AI stock phrases about shadows, echoes, nameless dread, humming air, impossible angles, etc.
- gratuitously abstract language
- overtly stating emotions characters/claimants have and what they are
- reader-visible copy that sounds like a prototype, debug panel, or generated placeholder

## 9. Revision Heuristics

Reject or rewrite any passage that:

- could belong to any claimant
- explains instead of dramatizes
- sounds impressive but changes nothing
- adds mood without pressure
- summarizes an artifact instead of letting the object do the work
- pre-renders a closed choice as if it were a full live branch
- keeps claimant focus high while leaving diction, cadence, and structure basically neutral
- mistakes illegibility for successful contamination
