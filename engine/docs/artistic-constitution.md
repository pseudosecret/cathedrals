# Artistic Constitution

## 1. Identity

This project is a static, build-time-generated literary artwork that combines nonlinear mystery, artifact browsing, and ontological judgment. It should feel like a literary escape room, not a normal novel, not a normal CYOA, and not a puzzle wrapper around weak prose.

## 2. Necessary Pillars

1. Nonlinear mystery narrative
2. Static-site artifact experience
3. AI-assisted generation and coherence pipeline

All three pillars must remain present. If one disappears, the work has become a different thing.

## 3. Artistic Goals

- The work must stand on its own as art.
- AI must function as medium and scaling instrument, not shortcut.
- The work should feel architecturally dense rather than merely branchy.
- The work should create a finite illusion of infinity.
- Choices should feel consequential, implicating, and costly.

## 4. Reader Effects

The reader should feel:

- intrigued
- pressured
- increasingly complicit
- uncertain whether they are investigating or judging
- aware that interpretation may be causal

## 5. Anti-Goals

The project must not become:

- shallow branching slop
- puzzle garnish on top of atmospheric mush
- lore dumping or telling instead of showing
- software overengineering that kills the art
- AI prose that sounds generic, inflated, or faux-profound

## 6. AI Role

The human authors:

- doctrine
- ontology
- state model
- arc grammar
- style law
- acceptance criteria
- claimant structure

AI authors:

- non-claimant characters
- claimant instantiations
- scenes
- artifacts
- local phrasing
- variant expressions within constraints

AI must not invent new metaphysics, claimants, or core truths.

## 7. MVP Boundary

The MVP is one short work with:

- one surface setting
- three claimants
- one central transgression
- one hidden contamination route
- static build only

## 8. Success Criteria

The MVP succeeds if:

- accusation visibly alters reality
- claimant routes feel genuinely different
- indecision has observable cost
- at least one hidden route reframes reader behavior
- the prose is good enough that the work would still matter if AI involvement were unknown
